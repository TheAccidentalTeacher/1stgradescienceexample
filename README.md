## Oklahoma 1st Grade Science Standards Alignment

This curriculum addresses the **2020 Oklahoma Academic Standards for Science (OAS-S)** for 1st Grade. Oklahoma's standards are based on Next Generation Science Standards but customized for Oklahoma students.

### **Physical Science: Waves (Light & Sound)**
- **1.PS4.1**: Plan and conduct investigations to provide evidence that vibrating materials can make sound and that sound can make materials vibrate
- **1.PS4.2**: Make observations to construct an evidence-based account that objects can be seen only when illuminated
- **1.PS4.3**: Plan and conduct an investigation to determine the effect of placing objects made with different materials in the path of a beam of light
- **1.PS4.4**: Use tools and materials to design and build a device that uses light or sound to solve the problem of communicating over a distance*

### **Life Science: Structures, Processes & Heredity**
- **1.LS1.1**: Use materials to design a solution to a human problem by mimicking how plants and/or animals use their external parts to help them survive, grow, and meet their needs*
- **1.LS1.2**: Obtain information from media or text to determine patterns in the behavior of parents and offspring that help offspring survive
- **1.LS3.1**: Make observations to construct an evidence-based account that young plants and animals are like, but not exactly like, their parents

### **Earth & Space Science: Patterns & Human Impact**
- **1.ESS1.1**: Use observations of the sun, moon, and stars to describe patterns that can be predicted
- **1.ESS1.2**: Make observations at different times of the year to relate the amount of daylight and relative temperature to the time of year
- **1.ESS3.1**: Communicate solutions that will reduce the impact of humans on the land, water, air, and/or other living things in the local environment*

### **Engineering, Technology & Applications (K-2)**
- **K-2.ETS1.1**: Ask questions, make observations, and gather information about a situation people want to change; define a simple problem that can be solved through development of a new or improved object or tool
- **K-2.ETS1.2**: Develop a simple sketch, drawing, or physical model to illustrate how the shape of an object helps it function as needed to solve a problem
- **K-2.ETS1.3**: Analyze data from tests of two objects designed to solve the same problem to compare strengths and weaknesses of how each performs

**Note:** *Asterisk indicates standards that incorporate engineering practices into science content

**Total Coverage:** 13 Performance Expectations (10 grade-specific + 3 K-2 engineering standards)

## Lesson Format

Each lesson follows this structure:

### 1. **Picture Time** (5 minutes)
Large, colorful image related to the topic. Bobby describes what he sees.

### 2. **Bible Time** (5 minutes)
Read short ESV passage (parent reads, or simplified for Bobby). Connect to lesson.

### 3. **Learn Time** (10-15 minutes)
Simple explanation with:
- 2-3 short sentences per concept
- Large font, simple words
- Multiple images/diagrams
- Word bank for new vocabulary

### 4. **Activity Time** (15-20 minutes)
Hands-on activity:
- Experiments
- Art projects
- Building/construction
- Movement activities
- Nature observation

### 5. **Review Time** (5 minutes)
Quick questions, show-and-tell, or drawing what we learned

### 6. **Prayer Time** (2 minutes)
Thank God for what we learned about His creation

## Parent Notes

### **Working with Bobby's Energy:**
- Start with movement activity to get wiggles out
- Build in "truck breaks" - let him play with trucks for 3-5 minutes
- Use outdoor time whenever possible
- Let him handle objects, touch textures
- Channel energy into activities (jumping, building, digging)

### **Building Reading Skills:**
- Point to words as you read
- Use same vocabulary words repeatedly
- Create picture-word cards
- Let Bobby "read" by describing images
- Celebrate small reading successes

### **Addressing Bug Aversion:**
- Start with pictures only
- Move to videos (safe distance)
- Eventually progress to observation containers
- Never force, but gently encourage
- Emphasize "brave explorer" identity
- Connect to brave Bible characters

### **Reinforcing YEC Worldview:**
- Consistently refer to "when God made..." not "millions of years ago"
- Use terms like "created kinds" instead of "evolution"
- Connect fossils to the Flood
- Emphasize humans and dinosaurs together
- Reference Biblical timeline (approx. 6,000 years)
